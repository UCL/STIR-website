Publications related to STIR

The main publication describing STIR is:

Kris Thielemans, Charalampos Tsoumpas, Sanida Mustafovic, Tobias Beisel, Pablo Aguiar, Nikolaos Dikaios, and Matthew W Jacobson,
STIR: Software for Tomographic Image Reconstruction Release 2,
Physics in Medicine and Biology, 57 (4), 2012 pp.867-883.
Please refer to this paper if you use STIR.

If you use STIR for SPECT, please also refer to

BM Fuster, C Falcon, C Tsoumpas, L Livieratos, P Aguiar, A Cot, D Ros, K Thielemans,
Integration of advanced 3D SPECT modeling into the open-source STIR framework,
Medical Physics 40 (9), 092502, 2013.

Below is a hand-crafted list of papers that describe STIR. There is also a corresponding (and more useful) database on CiteULike. If you use a specific STIR feature described in one of these papers, a reference would be appreciated.

A (probably incomplete) list of references to papers that use STIR is available on this CiteULike page. There's a Wiki page with instructions on how to add your own paper to those lists.

Journal papers describing/evaluating STIR (components)

  • Jacobson M, Levkovitz R, Ben-Tal A, Thielemans K, Spinks T, Belluzzo D, Pagani E, Bettinardi V, Gilardi M C, Zverovich A and Mitra G Enhanced 3D PET OSEM Reconstruction using inter-update Metz filters, Phys. Med. Biol. 45 No.8 (2000) 2417-2439) .
    This paper describes an alternative regularisation method for EMML using inter-update filtering (which is somewhat similar to inter-iteration filtering). It also describes the subset scheme used by STIR and gives some details on the parallelisation scheme used during PARAPET (which formed the basis for the current STIR MPI work).
  • V. Bettinardi, E. Pagani, M.C. Gilardi, S. Alenius, K. Thielemans, M. Teras, and F. Fazio, Implementation and evaluation of a 3D One Step Late reconstruction algorithm for 3D Positron Emission Tomography studies using Median Root Prior, Eur. J. Nucl. Med. (2002) Vol 29 No. 1 pp 7-18).
    MRP using STIR.
  • C. Tsoumpas, F. Turkheimer, K. Thielemans, Study of direct and indirect parametric estimation methods of linear models in dynamic positron emission tomography, Med. Phys. Volume 35, Issue 4, (2008) pp. 1299-1309)
  • Georgios I. Angelis, Kris Thielemans, Andri C. Tziortzi, Federico E. Turkheimer and Charalampos Tsoumpas, Convergence Optimization of Parametric MLEM Reconstruction for Estimation of Patlak Plot Parameters, Journal Computerized Medical Imaging and Graphics (Feb 2011)
    This study reveals how direct iterative reconstruction of kinetic parameters from the measured (FDOPA) data can be performed optimally (using STIR release 2.1). It explains how to optimize the speed of the algorithm minimizing bias; and it compares the results with analytic reconstructions.
  • Polycarpou I; Thielemans K; Manjeshwar R; Aguiar P; Marsden PK; Tsoumpas C Comparative evaluation of scatter correction in 3D PET using different scatter-level approximations, Annals of Nuclear Medicine (July 2011)
    This paper describes how to perform scatter correction using STIR and it evaluates its accuracy versus SimSET scatter estimation. Results demonstrate that in many cases scaling the single scatter distribution is a reasonable approximation to compensate for total scatter, especially if scaling is based on a global fit.
  • Kris Thielemans, Charalampos Tsoumpas, Sanida Mustafovic, Tobias Beisel, Pablo Aguiar, Nikolaos Dikaios, and Matthew W Jacobson, STIR: Software for Tomographic Image Reconstruction Release 2, Physics in Medicine and Biology, 57 (4), 2012 pp.867-883.
  • Polycarpou I; Tsoumpas C; Marsden PK (Oct 2012) Analysis and comparison of two methods for motion correction in PET imaging. Medical Physics. 39(10): 6474-6483.
  • BM Fuster, C Falcon, C Tsoumpas, L Livieratos, P Aguiar, A Cot, D Ros, K Thielemans, Integration of advanced 3D SPECT modeling into the open-source STIR framework, Medical Physics 40 (9), 092502, 2013.
  • Tsoumpas C; Polycarpou I; Thielemans K; Buerger C; King AP; Schaeffter T; Marsden PK (Mar 2013) The effect of regularization in motion compensated PET image reconstruction: A realistic numerical 4D simulation study. Physics in Medicine and Biology, 58(6):1759-1773.
    We have performed two realistic numerical 4D simulation study to compare the two main approaches for PET motion-compensation: motion correction within reconstruction versus post-reconstruction (Polycarpou et al, 2012) including median-root-prior regularisation (Tsoumpas, et al 2013) . Both techniques are based on the same iterative reconstruction algorithm in STIR (i.e. OSMAPOSL) approaches are available in the 2.4 release.
