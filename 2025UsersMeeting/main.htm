Where and when

This year the event will take place on Thursday, 6 November, 18:30-21:00 in Yokohama, Japan during the IEEE NSS-MIC. You can find more information on the venue at the conference's main website.

Overview

This year is the 25th anniversary of STIR, and for this occasion we are organising a special edition of the STIR Users' and Developers' Meeting. This year there will be no abstract submission, instead we are conducting a short survey where you have the possibility to choose a topic of your interest.

Attendees will be sorted in groups based on the survey and will participate in a selected demo and discussion. You will also be able to volunteer for a short presentation (5 min) on your recent work using STIR.

Fill in the survey!

Remote attendance will be possible, with one of the discussion groups dedicated to online participants. Please confirm your remote attendance by email to Daniel Deidda. In addition, an invite has been sent to the STIR and SyneRBI mailing lists.

Registration

Attendance is free of charge, but if you wish to reserve space and a dinner box you will need to register your attendance via the official IEEE NSS/MIC registration to the event with a small additional fee.

Schedule

We will cover the following 5 themes:
  • Reconstructing Siemens data with STIR
  • Using PET Monte Carlo software (GATE and SimSET) with STIR
  • Reconstructing SPECT real data and SIMIND simulations
  • [Online] Using the Python interface for STIR for rapid development of new workflows
  • Development of new algorithms in STIR
Tentative Agenda:
  • Welcome
  • Intro talks according to themes above
  • Break
  • Group discussions
  • Reports of group discussions
  • Future developments
