STIR User's and Developer's Meeting 2025

Organisers

  • Daniel Deidda, National Physical Laboratory, UK
  • Nikos Efthimiou, University of Groningen, Nederland
  • Charalampos Tsoumpas, University of Groningen, Nederland
  • Kris Thielemans, University College London, UK

Where and when

This year the event will take place on Thursday, 6 November, 18:00-20:00 in Yokohama, Japan during the IEEE NSS-MIC. You can find more information on the venue at the conference's main website.

Overview

This year is the 25th anniversary of STIR, and for this occasion we are organising a special edition of the STIR Users' and Developers' Meeting. This year there will be no abstract submission, instead we are conducting a short survey where you have the possibility to choose a topic of your interest.

Attendees will be sorted in groups based on the survey and will participate in a selected demo and discussion. You will also be able to volunteer for a short presentation (5 min) on your recent work using STIR.

Fill in the survey!

We anticipate that remote attendance will be possible, with one of the discussion groups dedicated to online participants. Please confirm your remote attendance by email to Daniel Deidda. In addition, an invite will be sent to the STIR and SyneRBI mailing lists at later date.

We look forward to meeting you in Yokohama!
Daniel, Nikos, Harry and Kris

(For up-to-date information on this meeting please always check the STIR website.)

Registration

Attendance is free of charge, but if you wish to reserve space and a dinner box you will need to register your attendance via the official IEEE NSS/MIC registration to the event with a small additional fee. We encourage you to reach out to us to discuss your topic. We are hoping that, similarly to last years, remote presentation/attendance will be possible.

Tentative schedule

We will cover the following 5 themes:
  • Reconstructing Siemens data
  • PET Monte Carlo
  • Reconstructing real data and SIMIND simulations for SPECT
  • Development of new algorithms in STIR
  • [Online] Using the Python interface for STIR for rapid development of new workflows
Tentative Agenda:
  • Welcome
  • Intro talks according to themes above
  • Break
  • Group discussions
  • Reports of group discussions
  • Future developments
